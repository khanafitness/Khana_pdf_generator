Iran. Operation Sindoor. Oil. Three wars. One target — your salary.

What Is Inflation? Inflation simply means things are getting costlier over time. Your money buys less every year. The ₹500 you spent on groceries in 2023 now costs you ₹710 — for the exact same items. No money left for your future or even an emergency.

Chapter Two
How Inflation Is Measured
The numbers that tell you how bad the war really is — and why the official number lies to you.

The government releases an inflation number every month. The news covers it. RBI reacts to it. But most people don't know what it actually measures — or why the number on TV feels nothing like the number in your grocery bag.

Important: CPI is always released with a one-month lag — around the 12th of every month. The number you read in the news today reflects last month's prices, not this month. Released monthly by MoSPI. Next release: ~12th of every month.

⚠️ India's New CPI Series — January 2026: India launched a completely new CPI measurement system in January 2026. Base year changed from 2012 to 2024. Food weight in the basket was reduced by nearly 9 percentage points. New categories added — OTT subscriptions, e-commerce, digital services. The official inflation number will look lower going forward. Your actual grocery bill won't.

The Main Metrics — Explained
CPI — Consumer Price Index

The most important number for you. Tracks price changes in a basket of everyday household items — food, vegetables, milk, transport, clothing, rent, medicine. This is what the news reports. This is what RBI targets. April 2026: CPI = 3.48% (new 2024-base series). Rural inflation 3.74%, urban 3.16%.

WPI — Wholesale Price Index

Tracks prices at the factory and mandi level — before goods reach you. Think: price of wheat at a mandi, price of steel at a factory gate. WPI moves first. CPI follows. If WPI is rising today — your grocery bill rises in 2–3 months. It is your early warning system.

Food Inflation — CFPI

Tracked separately as the Consumer Food Price Index. Food is 40%+ of how most Indian households actually spend money — even though the new CPI basket has reduced its official weight. April 2026: Food inflation = 4.20%, up from 3.87% in March. Rising for 6 consecutive months.

Core Inflation

CPI minus food and fuel. Removes seasonal noise — vegetable prices after a bad monsoon, petrol prices after a war. Core inflation shows the underlying structural trend. This is what RBI actually watches most closely when deciding whether to raise or cut interest rates.

The official number looks good. Your grocery bill doesn't.04

RBI's Role — How India Fights Inflation

RBI's official inflation target — between 2% and 6%. Below 2% risks deflation. Above 6% — RBI must act. It has three main tools:

TOOL1
Repo Rate
The rate at which RBI lends to banks. RBI raises repo rate → banks raise loan rates → your home loan EMI goes up → people borrow and spend less → demand falls → prices cool. Current repo rate: 5.25%. When you hear "RBI hiked rates" — your EMI is about to go up.
TOOL2
CRR — Cash Reserve Ratio
Every bank must keep a % of deposits with RBI as cash. RBI raises CRR → banks have less money to lend → credit in economy shrinks → spending falls → inflation cools.
TOOL3
SLR — Statutory Liquidity Ratio
Banks must invest a % of deposits in government securities. Raised during high inflation to reduce the money available for lending into the economy.

The Key Insight: RBI projects inflation at 4.6% for FY2026-27 — before the full impact of the oil shock. HSBC's post-shock forecast suggests inflation could breach the 6% upper band. When that happens — rate hikes follow. Your EMIs go up. Plan accordingly.

RBI targets 4%. HSBC forecasts 4.6%+. Hope for 4%. Plan for 8%.05

Chapter Three
How War Kills Your Salary Silently
The 5 silent killers eating your purchasing power every month

You now know what inflation is and how it is measured. But knowing the number is not the same as feeling it. This chapter shows you where it actually hits — in your petrol tank, your grocery bag, your rent, your pay slip, and your tax deduction. Five silent killers. Happening every single month.

The 5 Silent Killers in 2026
Killer 1 — Transport Costs

Petrol prices are directly linked to crude oil. Every ₹10 rise in petrol costs the average bike owner ₹300–400 extra per month. Car owners — ₹800–1,200 extra. But it doesn't stop at your vehicle. Every delivery on Blinkit, Zepto, Swiggy — that delivery person's fuel cost went up. That cost is passed to you.

⚠️ Petrol Has Already Crossed ₹100 in Delhi — May 2026. After a four-year price freeze, oil marketing companies raised petrol and diesel prices four times in 10 days in May 2026. Delhi petrol hit ₹102.12/litre. Diesel ₹95.20/litre. CNG crossed ₹80/kg for the first time. Under-recoveries had hit ₹1,000 crore daily — the dam finally broke. More hikes are possible if crude stays above $110.

Killer 2 — Food Prices

Vegetables in India move by truck. Trucks run on diesel. Diesel is expensive. But there's a second hit coming — fertiliser. Oil is used to make urea. When oil prices rise, urea prices rise. When urea prices rise — wheat, rice, dal, vegetables all cost more to grow. Your dal-chawal is now a casualty of the Middle East war.

Killer 3 — The Rent Trap

Rent rises because landlords have expenses too — maintenance, property tax, their own rising costs. In cities where jobs are — Mumbai, Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad, Delhi — housing demand never drops. Average rent increases in Tier 1 cities in 2026 — 8 to 12%. Your salary increase — 5 to 8%. Rent is already winning.

Your salary is losing ground every single month.06

Two More Dangers to Know
Deflation
When Prices Fall — Sounds Good. Actually Dangerous.

If prices are falling — people wait to buy (why buy today if it's cheaper tomorrow?). Businesses sell less. Cut jobs. People earn less. Spend even less. Prices fall more. A vicious cycle. Japan lost three decades to deflation. Low growth, no wealth creation, stagnation for an entire generation.

Stagflation — 2026 India Risk
High Inflation + Low Growth Simultaneously

Normally RBI can cool inflation by raising rates. But stagflation means the economy is already weak. Raise rates → economy gets worse. Cut rates → inflation gets worse. RBI is stuck with no good move. India's 2026 risk: oil shock slowing growth + food and fuel prices rising = stagflation is a real possibility. Not confirmed. But watch for it.

The question is not whether inflation is coming. The question is which side are you on.09

What Is Actually Worrying You
The 6 fears every salaried Indian carries — but nobody talks about

Let's stop talking about oil prices and rupees for a moment. Let's talk about you. Because behind every financial decision — every SIP you started and stopped, every time you said "I'll start investing next month" — there is a feeling. Not a number. A feeling.

The Salaried Person's Trap: It's not really inflation that keeps you up at night. It's what inflation represents. The fear that no matter how hard you work — it won't be enough.

The 6 Fears
Fear 1 — "What if I lose my job?"

You don't say this out loud. But you think it. Companies are cutting costs. AI is replacing roles. India-Pakistan tension rattled markets. When businesses hurt — the first thing they cut is people. The best job security in the world is having 6 months of expenses saved. Not because it helps you keep your job. Because it means losing your job isn't a disaster anymore.

Fear 2 — "My EMIs are eating me alive"

You took a home loan. Or a car loan. Or both. At the time it made sense. But now petrol is expensive. Groceries cost more. And that EMI sits there every month — fixed, unmovable, unforgiving. It doesn't care that onions are ₹80/kg now. Chapter 09 will show you exactly how to restructure around it.

Fear 3 — "My parents are getting older"

Nobody talks about this in finance books. But every Indian salaried person between 28 and 42 carries it. Medical costs in India are rising at 10–14% every year. A single hospitalisation — heart, kidney, ortho — can cost ₹3 to ₹8 lakh easily. One health insurance policy for your parents — ₹15,000 to ₹25,000 per year — can protect ₹5 to ₹10 lakh of medical costs.

Every fear has the same root cause — no financial structure. That changes today.10

What NOT To Do Right Now
6 dangerous mistakes salaried Indians make when fear takes over

Fear makes people do stupid things with money. Not because they're stupid. Because nobody told them what to do — so they do what feels safe. And in a war — what feels safe is usually the most dangerous thing you can do.

The 6 Costly Mistakes
Mistake 1 — Stopping Your SIP

Markets fall. News is scary. Your SIP statement shows red. So you stop it — "just for a few months." Things never settle. You never restart. When markets fall — your SIP buys MORE units for the same money. A market crash is a tomato sale. Your SIP automatically buys more when prices are low. The people who kept SIPs running through Corona March 2020 doubled their money by 2022.

Mistake 2 — Putting Everything In FD

Best FD rate today — 7.5%. After tax (20-30% bracket) — 5.25% to 6%. Inflation coming in FY27 — RBI projects 4.6%, food inflation already at 4.2% in April 2026 and rising for 6 straight months. Your real return from FD — 0% to 1.5%. You're not growing your money. You're barely keeping it alive.

Mistake 3 — Buying Physical Gold Right Now

Gold has already run up significantly. Everyone who should have bought gold — already bought it. Now you're buying at a high price. Paying GST. Paying making charges. Storing it. Worrying about theft. And physical gold gives you zero income while you hold it. There is a better way to own gold in 2026 — Sovereign Gold Bonds. Same exposure + 2.5% interest + zero GST + tax free at maturity. Chapter 08 explains this.

Most people keep emergency money in a savings account at 3.5%. Inflation is 3.4% today. Rising tomorrow. Your emergency fund is already losing. Liquid mutual funds invest in government securities and high quality short term bonds. Completely safe. Returns — 6.5 to 7.5% currently. Your money is available within 24 hours. Same safety. W2Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs) — The Smartest Way to Own Gold

SGBs are issued by the Reserve Bank of India. You get: gold price appreciation + 2.5% annual interest paid every 6 months + zero GST + zero storage cost + completely tax free at maturity (8 years).

⚠️ Honest Warning: Gold is currently near all-time highs. Buying SGBs at peak prices = limited upside in short term. If the war ends in 1–2 years, gold could fall 15–25%. On May 11, 2026 — PM Modi publicly asked Indians to pause gold purchases. When the PM says don't buy gold right now — that's your clearest signal to wait. Buy on a 10–15% correction.

Timing Matters — What To Buy When

The eight weapons above are not all equal at all times. Buying the right weapon at the wrong time reduces your returns significantly.

Market SituationWhat To Do
Gold at all-time highWait. Small SGB only if long-term war view. Buy on 10–15% dip.
Gold falls 15–25%Strong SGB buying opportunity. Increase allocation.
Stock market at all-time highKeep SIP running. Don't lump sum. Rupee cost averaging protects you.
Stock market crashes 20–30%Increase SIP amount aggressively. This is your Corona moment.
Rupee weakening sharplyIncrease international fund allocation. Dollar assets protect you.
Interest rates fallingREITs and bonds more attractive. Good time to add.
War escalates furtherDefence funds. Small SGB. Reduce luxury spending.
War ends suddenlyReduce gold. Increase equity. Markets typically rally on peace.

One Golden Rule: Never stop your index fund SIP. Regardless of market conditions. Everything else — time intelligently. Index fund SIP — runs always. Through war, peace, crash, rally. That one habit alone builds more wealth than any timing strategy ever will.

Chapter Nine
The War Budget
The 3-bucket system that makes wealth building automatic

⚠️ The Backwards Order Most People Follow: Lifestyle first → Bills second → EMIs third → Whatever is left — save. This is completely backwards. And it's why most people with decent salaries have nothing to show after 10 years.

✅ The War Budget Order: Invest first → Protect second → Live on the rest. Pay yourself first. This one change is the single biggest shift between people who build wealth and people who wonder where it went.

The 3 Bucket War System
01
SURVIVE — Non-negotiable. Fixed. Untouchable.
Rent/EMI 20–30% · Groceries 8–10% · Transport 5–7% · Utilities 3–5% · Insurance 3–5% · Family support 5–10%
02
PROTECT & GROW — Your future. Paid first. Minimum 20%.
Emergency liquid fund · Index fund SIP · NPS · Child education SIP · SGBs / REITs / Defence funds
03
LIVE — Everything else. Spend completely. Zero guilt.
Whatever remains after Bucket 1 and 2 — is yours. Spend it fully. Enjoy it fully.

⚠️ If Bucket 2 is below 20%: Don't panic. Even 5% or 10% is a start. The goal is to increase it by 1% every 6 months — through a raise, a cut in Bucket 3, or removing one unnecessary Bucket 1 expense. You don't need to be perfect. You need to start and keep moving.

The Automation Rule: Set up standing instructions from your salary account on day 1 or 2 of every month — all Bucket 2 investments transfer automatically. What remains is Bucket 1 and 3. You don't need discipline to not spend Bucket 2. It's already gone. Invested. Working.
